ControlLight: Towards Controllable, Consistent, and Generalizable Low-Light Enhancement

ControlLight studies low-light enhancement from three practical axes: controllable illumination strength, consistent content preservation, and generalizable performance across diverse real-world scenes.

Overview

What ControlLight emphasizes

Controllable

A unified scalar control allows continuous transitions from the original low-light input to progressively enhanced outputs, making intensity adjustment explicit and easy to inspect.

Consistent

The method aims to preserve identity, scene geometry, and local structures throughout enhancement, reducing unnecessary content drift across different illumination strengths.

Generalizable

The same framework is showcased on indoor, street, rainy-night, portrait, group, and backlit examples to highlight robust behavior across diverse real-image conditions.
